Mac and Cheese

Description

Little needs to be said about the legendary side dish. Although I will remind you that it should be plenty cheesy, plenty creamy and should posess a nice layer of crisp over the top. With this recipe, there is no need for boxed mac and cheese anymore. You need this, whether at your next get together or dinner at home.

Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter: 1 stick
  • Salt: to taste
  • Elbow macaroni: 1 lb
  • Flour: 1/2 cup
  • Whole milk: 5 cups
  • Mustard powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Pepper: to taste
  • Shredded cheddar: 12 oz
  • Shredded Gruyere: 8 oz
  • Grated Parmesan: 3 oz
  • Panko bread crumbs: 1 cup
  • Olive oil: 3 tablespoons

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 and coat your baking dish with butter.
  2. In a pot of boiling salted water, cook your macaroni until al dente and drain the water.
  3. Melt your stick of butter in a pan over medium heat and then sprinkle your flour over the top. Stir for 2 to 3 minutes or until golden.
  4. Add your milk and whisk until even. Now add mustard powder, salt and pepper. Make sure heat is still medium and continue to stir until the sauce thickens.
  5. Remove the pan from the heat. Now add your cheddar, Gruyere and approximately 70% of your Parmesan and whisk until cheese is melted into the sauce.
  6. Stir in the macaroni and transfer to buttered baking dish.
  7. Using a small bowl, mix your bread crumbs and oil with your leftover Parmesan. Sprinkle this mixture over the top of the macaroni and season to your liking.
  8. Bake your mac and cheese until golden, I would recommend around 30 minutes and another 10 to sit and cool.
