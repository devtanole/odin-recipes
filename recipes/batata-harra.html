Batata Harra

Description

Batata harra is a spicy potato dish native to Lebanon and some parts of the Middle East. Potatoes are diced and fried with red peppers, coriander, chili and garlic all together in olive oil, tomato paste and lemon juice. Served plain or with pita.

How does batata harra taste?

This is a very flavorful dish that should be described as more than spicy. Citrusy, tangy and fresh all fit the bill. Each ingredient works together in harmony to delight whoever partakes. This dish is packed with flavor and the moderate spice is enough to enjoy the flavor and still enjoy other foods with it.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes: I recommend cubing 3 Russet potatoes.
  • Garlic: 2 teaspoons minced
  • Tomato paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Red chili flakes: 1 teaspoon
  • Paprika: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Vegetable oil: 3 tablespoons
  • Coriander: 2 tablespoons chopped
  • Olive oil: 1 tablespoon
  • Unsalted butter: 1 tablespoon
  • Cumin: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Black pepper and salt: to taste
  • Lemon juice: 3 tablespoons

Steps

  1. Add your butter and olive oil to a saucepan over medium heat. Then add your garlic and tomato paste to the pan and cook for around 4 minutes.
  2. Once your tomato paste darkens from red to a red-brown, add your chili flakes, paprika, cumin and stir together well.
  3. Now add the coriander and lemon juice. Stir the mixture until a sauce forms.
  4. Taste and adjust to your liking. Perhaps you could add the salt and pepper.
  5. Soak potatoes in ice cold water for 10 minutes then cube once the time is up.
  6. Add cubed potatoes to boiling water for 5 minutes then drain and pat dry.
  7. Evenly coat potatoes in cornstarch.
  8. Heat up pan with vegetable oil to 350 degrees Farenheit. Add potatoes and fry until golden brown.
  9. Move potatoes to pan with previously made sauce and toss until coated well.
  10. Enjoy!
